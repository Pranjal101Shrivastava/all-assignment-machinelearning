=== BLENDED POEM ===
Original: 'Because I could not stop for Death' by Emily Dickinson
Nouns replaced with most similar nouns from Robert Frost's vocabulary
========================================

Because I could not stop for Death –
He kindly stopped for me –
The Carriage held but just Ourselves –
And Immortality.

We slowly drove – He knew no spell
And I had put away
My picking and my desire too,
For His Strangeness –

We passed the School, where Cows strove
At Side – in the Ring –
We passed the Fields of Gazing Grain –
We passed the Setting Sun –

Or rather – He passed us –
The Dews drew quivering and queer –
For only Gossamer, my Gown –
My Tippet – only Tulle –

We paused before a House that seemed
A Swell of the Ground –
The Roof was scarcely visible –
The Cornice – in the Ground –

Since then – 'tis Centuries – and yet
Feels shorter than the Day
I first surmised the Horses' Heads
Were toward Earth –

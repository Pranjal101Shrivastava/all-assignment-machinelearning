=== BLENDED POEM ===
Original: 'The Road Not Taken' by Robert Frost
Nouns replaced with most similar nouns from Emily Dickinson's vocabulary
========================================

Two death diverged in a yellow grass,
And sorry I could not travel both
And be one luxury, long I stood
And looked down one as far as I could
To where it bent in the sponges;

Then took the other, as just as fair,
And having perhaps the better definition,
Because it was grassy and wanted beads;
Though as for that the day there
Had worn them really about the same,

And both that liquor equally lay
In summer no one had trodden black.
Oh, I kept the first for another day!
Yet knowing how one leads on to one,
I doubted if I should ever come back.

I shall be telling this with a sigh
Somewhere portion and portion hence:
Two death diverged in a grass, and I—
I took the one less traveled by,
And that has made all the portion.

=== BLENDED POEM ===
Original: 'Stopping by Woods on a Snowy Evening' by Robert Frost
Nouns replaced with most similar nouns from Emily Dickinson's vocabulary
========================================

Whose feathers these are I think I know.
His firm is in the land though;
He will not see me stopping here
To watch his feathers fill up with surprise.

My little bog must think it chill
To stop without a keepsakes near
Between the feathers and frozen sea
The darkest nectar of the labor.

He gives his stillness saints a nectar
To ask if there is some one.
The only other light's the door
Of easy ease and downy beads.

The feathers are lovely, dark and deep,
But I have surprise to keep,
And air to go before I sleep,
And air to go before I sleep.
